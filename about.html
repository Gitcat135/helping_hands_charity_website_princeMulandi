About Helping Hands

Our story, mission, and the team behind our work

Our Story

Helping Hands was founded in 2010 by Sarah Gakuo, a former teacher who witnessed firsthand the educational disparities in developing countries during her travels.

What began as a small initiative to build one school in a rural village in Kenya has grown into an international organization operating in 12 countries across three continents.

Over the past decade, we've helped over 1,200 children access quality education, trained hundreds of teachers, and built 35 schools in communities that previously had no educational infrastructure.

Our Mission

To provide quality education to underprivileged children worldwide, breaking the cycle of poverty through learning and empowerment.

We believe education is the most powerful tool for creating lasting change in individuals, families, and communities.

Our Vision

A world where every child, regardless of their background or circumstances, has access to quality education that enables them to reach their full potential.

We envision communities where education is valued and accessible to all, creating a ripple effect of positive change across generations.

Our Values

Compassion

We approach our work with empathy and understanding, recognizing the dignity and potential in every child we serve.

Integrity

We maintain the highest ethical standards, ensuring transparency in our operations and accountability for every dollar donated.

Collaboration

We work in partnership with local communities, governments, and other organizations to create sustainable solutions.
