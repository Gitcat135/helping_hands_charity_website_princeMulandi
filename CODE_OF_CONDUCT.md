# Code of Conduct for The Development Of The Helping Hands Charity Website

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Respect**: Treating all individuals with respect, empathy, and consideration.
- **Constructive Feedback**: Using welcoming and inclusive language, being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- **Collaboration**: Focusing on what is best for the community, not just what benefits us individually.
- **Accountability**: Owning up to mistakes and learning from them.
- **Safety**: Ensuring personal and collective safety in both digital and physical spaces related to our project.

**Unacceptable behaviors** include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Responsibilities

Project [maintainers](#maintainers) are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community. Examples of representing our project include using an official project e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project team at [support@bolderlearner.com](support@bolderlearner.com). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

- **Immediate Response**: The project team will respond to reports of violations in a timely manner.
- **Consequences**: Consequences may include warnings, temporary or permanent bans from the project or associated events, depending on the severity of the violation.

## Resolutions

The [maintainers](#maintainers) must agree on a resolution by consensus. If the maintainers cannot reach consensus and deadlocks for over a week, the maintainers will turn the matter over to the management of Bolder Learner Tech School for resolution.

**Possible responses** may include:

- Taking no further action (if we determine no violation occurred).
- A private reprimand from the maintainers to the individual(s) involved. In this case, a maintainer will deliver that reprimand to the individual(s) over email, cc'ing the other maintainers.
- A public reprimand. In this case, a maintainer will deliver that reprimand in the same venue that the violation occurred (i.e. in-person for an in-person violation; email for an email violation, etc.). The maintainers may choose to publish this message elsewhere for posterity.
- An imposed vacation (i.e. asking someone to "take a week off" from a mailing list or formal or personal contact list). A maintainer will communicate this "vacation" to the individual(s). They'll be asked to take this vacation voluntarily, but if they don't agree then a temporary ban may be imposed to enforce this vacation.
- A permanent or temporary ban from some or all Physical Computing And Robotics spaces (mailing lists, chat groups, etc.). The maintainers will maintain records of all such bans so that they may be reviewed in the future, extended to new contributor, or otherwise maintained.
- A request for a public or private apology. A maintainer will deliver this request. The maintainers may, if it chooses, attach "strings" to this request: for example, the maintainer may ask a violator to apologize in order to retain his or her membership on a mailing list.

Once a resolution is agreed upon, but before it is enacted, the maintainers will contact the original reporter and any other affected parties and explain the proposed resolution. The maintainers will ask if this resolution is acceptable, and must note feedback for the record. However, the maintainers is not required to act on this feedback.

Finally the maintainers will make a report for the Bolder Learner Tech School management. In case the incident or report involves a current member of the management, the maintainers will provide the report only to the other management members.

The maintainers will never publicly discuss the issue; all public statements will be made by the Bolder Learner Tech School management.

## Conflicts of Interest

In the event of any conflict of interest a contributor must immediately notify the other contributors, and recuse themselves if necessary. If a report concerns a possible violation by a current contributor, this contributor should be excluded from the response process. For these cases, anyone can make a report directly to any of the [maintainers](#maintainers).
